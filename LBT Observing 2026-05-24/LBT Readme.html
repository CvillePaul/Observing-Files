Program Summary:
Targets are TESS identified eclipsing quadruple systems (2 sets of Eclipsing Binaries, each showing both a Primary and Secondary Eclipse).  

Our goal for this observing session is to obtain unambiguous radial velocity measurements for these systems.
We therefore seek observations when one of the two binary systems is in eclipse and the other is halfway between eclipses.

Instructions:
The binary systems in our targets have periods as short as several hours.
As such, we provide observing priority for each target in half hour increments, with more asterisks indicating higher priority.
For any given timeslot, please observe the target with the most asterisks, and avoid observing targets with no asterisks.
Repeat observations are acceptable as long as they are separated by more than 2 hours and as long as there are still asterisks in that time slot. 

Each science target has a corresponding RV standard of similar effective temperature and chosen to be within 30 degrees of the science target.
If possible, please obtain one exposure per run of the RV standards associated with each science target that has been observed.
